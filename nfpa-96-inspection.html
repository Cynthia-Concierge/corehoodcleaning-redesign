What is an NFPA 96 Inspection?

NFPA 96 is the Standard for Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations. It's the national fire code that governs how often your commercial kitchen exhaust system must be inspected and cleaned.

Why NFPA 96 Compliance Matters

San Diego commercial kitchens are required by law to maintain their exhaust systems according to NFPA 96 standards. A failed inspection can result in immediate shutdown, fines, insurance issues, and in extreme cases, fire hazards that put your staff and customers at risk.

What We Inspect:

  • ✓ Grease accumulation in hood, ducts, and exhaust fan
  • ✓ Proper operation of exhaust fan and makeup air systems
  • ✓ Clearances to combustible materials
  • ✓ Access panel installation and condition
  • ✓ Fire suppression system condition
  • ✓ Filter condition and proper installation
  • ✓ Hood construction and integrity
  • ✓ Grease containment and drainage

Inspection Frequency Requirements:

NFPA 96 specifies different cleaning frequencies based on cooking volume and fuel type:

  • ✓ Monthly: High-volume cooking (24-hour operations, charbroilers)
  • ✓ Quarterly: Moderate-volume cooking (most restaurants)
  • ✓ Semi-Annually: Low-volume operations (breakfast/lunch only)
  • ✓ Annually: Seasonal or limited use facilities

Common NFPA 96 Violations We Find

Excessive Grease Buildup

The #1 violation - grease accumulation beyond code limits in hoods, ducts, or fans. This is a critical fire hazard and requires immediate cleaning.

Inadequate Access Panels

Missing or improperly installed access panels prevent proper cleaning and inspection of ductwork.

Damaged or Missing Filters

Torn filters or gaps allow grease to bypass the filtration system and accumulate in ductwork faster.

Insufficient Clearances

Combustible materials (storage, decorations) placed too close to cooking equipment or exhaust hoods.
