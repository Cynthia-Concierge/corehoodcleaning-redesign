Chula Vista Fire Code Compliance & Health Inspections

Chula Vista restaurants fall under the Chula Vista Fire Department's jurisdiction, with five fire stations serving different areas of the city (Stations 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5). The San Diego County Department of Environmental Health conducts regular food facility inspections throughout Chula Vista, with particular attention to the high density of restaurants on Third Avenue and in major commercial centers.

What Chula Vista Health Inspectors Check:

  • Current hood cleaning certificate showing service within NFPA 96 required intervals
  • Before/after photos documenting complete system cleaning (hood, ductwork, exhaust fan)
  • Clean baffle filters with no excessive grease saturation or dripping
  • Hood interior surfaces free of dangerous grease buildup
  • Proper ductwork access panels for full-system cleaning verification
  • Working exhaust fan with adequate CFM for your cooking equipment
  • Fire suppression system with current annual inspection certification
  • Grease containment (no grease dripping onto cooking surfaces or floors)

Red Tag Emergencies: If San Diego County Health Department red-tags your Chula Vista kitchen for hood compliance violations, call us immediately at (858) 361-2570. We offer same-day emergency service throughout Chula Vista to get you back open and serving customers quickly. Insurance Requirements: Most Chula Vista commercial property leases and restaurant insurance policies require documented proof of regular hood cleaning. After every service, you'll receive a certificate of performance and complete photo documentation that satisfies insurance and property management requirements.
