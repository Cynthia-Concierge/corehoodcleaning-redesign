What is Commercial Hood Cleaning?

Commercial kitchen hood cleaning (also called exhaust hood cleaning or kitchen exhaust system cleaning) is the professional deep-cleaning of your restaurant's ventilation system. This includes the hood, filters, plenum, ductwork, and exhaust fan that remove smoke, steam, and grease-laden vapors from your cooking area.

Why Professional Hood Cleaning is Critical

Every time you cook, grease particles become airborne and travel through your exhaust system. Over time, this grease accumulates as a thick, highly flammable coating throughout your entire ventilation systemâ€”from the hood interior to the rooftop exhaust fan.

This grease buildup is the #1 cause of restaurant fires in the United States. According to NFPA (National Fire Protection Association), nearly 8,000 restaurant fires occur annually, with cooking equipment being the leading cause of ignition. Most of these fires could be prevented with proper hood cleaning.

In San Diego, the fire marshal enforces NFPA 96 standards, which mandate professional hood cleaning at specific intervals based on your cooking volume. Non-compliance can result in fines, failed health inspections, insurance issues, or in worst cases, a devastating fire that destroys your business.

The Core Hood Cleaning Process (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Pre-Service Preparation

We arrive on schedule (usually after hours or during slow periods). Our team lays down floor protection, moves equipment, and prepares the work area. We take comprehensive "before" photos of your entire system.

Step 2: Filter Removal & Hood Interior Cleaning

All baffle filters are removed and set aside for degreasing. We hand-scrape grease from hood interior surfaces, then pressure wash with heated water and commercial degreasers. All grease troughs, baffles, and hidden areas are addressed.

Step 3: Plenum & Ductwork Access

Using existing access panels (or creating temporary access if needed), our technicians enter the ductwork and hand-scrape grease from all accessible areas. We use specialized tools to reach difficult sections and ensure 100% grease removal from plenum to rooftop.

Step 4: Rooftop Exhaust Fan Cleaning

On the roof, we disassemble the exhaust fan, clean each blade individually, degrease the motor housing, and clean the fan housing interior. This ensures proper airflow and prevents grease from dripping back down into the ductwork.

Step 5: Filter Cleaning & Polishing

Filters are soaked in industrial degreaser, pressure washed, and dried. Hood exterior is polished to a professional shine. All equipment is reassembled and we verify proper fan operation.

Step 6: Final Inspection & Documentation

We take comprehensive "after" photos, complete your service certificate, upload everything to your customer portal, and walk through the results with your manager. We also provide recommendations for next service date based on NFPA 96 requirements.

How Often Should You Schedule Hood Cleaning?

NFPA 96 specifies minimum cleaning frequencies based on your cooking volume and fuel type. San Diego fire marshals enforce these standards strictly:

Monthly Cleaning Required

High-volume operations: 24-hour restaurants, charbroiler-heavy kitchens, wood-fired ovens, barbecue restaurants, high-volume fast food.

Quarterly Cleaning Required

Moderate-volume operations: Most full-service restaurants, hotels, casinos, hospitals, schools with daily lunch service.

Semi-Annual Cleaning Required

Low-volume operations: Breakfast/lunch only cafes, church kitchens, senior centers, light cooking operations, pizza shops (if no charbroiling).

Annual Cleaning Required

Minimal-use operations: Seasonal facilities, wedding venues with occasional use, community centers, facilities with electric cooking only.

Not sure which category you fall into? Call us at (858) 361-2570 for a free assessment. We'll evaluate your cooking equipment and volume to recommend the right cleaning frequency for compliance.

San Diego Fire Code & Health Department Requirements

San Diego County enforces California Fire Code and NFPA 96 standards for all commercial kitchens. Your restaurant is legally required to maintain documentation proving your hood system is cleaned according to these standards.

What San Diego Inspectors Look For:

  • Current hood cleaning certificate (must show service within required interval)
  • Before/after photos accessible via customer portal or physical records
  • Evidence of proper access panel installation for ductwork cleaning
  • Clean filters with no visible grease dripping
  • Hood interior free of excessive grease buildup
  • Proper clearances to combustible materials
  • Working fire suppression system with current inspection tag

Consequences of Non-Compliance: Failed health inspections can result in red tags (immediate closure), fines ranging from $500-$2,000+, insurance claim denials if a fire occurs, and in extreme cases, criminal liability if a fire causes injury.

Core Hood Cleaning provides all documentation needed for San Diego health inspections. Our certificates are accepted by all San Diego County health departments, fire marshals, and insurance companies.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hood Cleaning

How long does a hood cleaning service take?

Most standard restaurant hood systems take 2-4 hours to clean properly. Larger or more complex systems may take 4-6 hours. We typically schedule services during off-hours (late night or early morning) to avoid disrupting your business operations.

Do you offer same-day or emergency hood cleaning?

Yes! If you've received a red tag from the health department or fire marshal, or if you have a critical inspection coming up, call us at (858) 361-2570. We offer same-day emergency service throughout San Diego County for urgent situations.

What if I don't know when my hood was last cleaned?

This is common when restaurants change ownership or lose records. We'll perform a visual inspection during your free walkthrough. If grease buildup exceeds code limits, we'll recommend immediate cleaning. Once you're on our maintenance schedule, we track everything for you.

Can I clean my own hood to save money?

You can clean the visible hood surfaces yourself, but NFPA 96 requires professional cleaning of the entire system (including inaccessible ductwork and rooftop fan). DIY cleaning won't satisfy fire marshal or insurance requirements, and you won't have the certificate of performance needed for compliance.

What happens if I skip hood cleaning?

You risk failed health inspections (which can shut down your restaurant), insurance claim denials if a fire occurs, fines from the fire marshal, and most critically, a dangerous fire hazard that puts your staff, customers, and business at risk.
